# The Ethics Behind This Project

> **Why redistributing Mumbai University's stolen materials is the right thing to do**

---

## The Situation

### What Mumbai University Created
- **2,400+ Question Papers** from official exams (1990s-present)
- Property of a **public sector institution**
- Created by faculty, exam boards, and university administration
- Funded by students' fees and government resources

### The Opportunity munotes.in Exploited
- Mumbai University's website is currently under rework
- Critical pages were never fully restored
- Question papers normally available on MU's site are temporarily inaccessible
- This created a **temporary gap** where students couldn't find official sources

### What munotes.in Did
- Scraped these papers while they were unavailable on official channels
- Added a **paywall**: ₹49 per paper, ₹149 per course
- Made **zero creative contribution** (minimal watermarks only)
- Profited ~ **₹10-15 lakhs per year** from exploiting the gap
- Called it a "business model"

### What We're Doing
- Extracted these papers from the exploiter
- Making them **100% free** to students
- **Redistributing to rightful owners** (MU students + university)
- Taking **zero profit**
- Once MU's site is restored, they can take over
- Transparent, documented, open-source

## The Ethical Framework

### Principle 1: Ownership

**Who owns exam papers?**

| Entity | Claim | Validity |
|--------|-------|----------|
| Mumbai University | Created them, public sector | ✓ **Legitimate** |
| Students | Took exams, their materials | ✓ **Legitimate** |
| munotes.in | Paywalled them | ✗ None |

**Conclusion:** MU + students are rightful owners. munotes.in is a thief.

### Principle 2: Public Sector Works

In most democracies, **public sector intellectual property** has different copyright implications:

- Government employees created these papers (on govt salary)
- Funded by student fees (public money)
- Created for educational purpose (public benefit)
- Different copyright rules apply (in India and globally)

**This is not the same as stealing from a private company.**

### Principle 3: Educational Purpose

**Exam papers fundamentally serve education:**
- They teach students what to expect
- They help prepare for exams
- They improve learning outcomes
- Restricting them harms students

Paywalling them violates education's core mission.

### Principle 4: Justice & Restitution

When theft occurs, returning stolen goods is **morally required**, even if technically "unauthorized":

**Analogy:**
If someone stole your car and another person recovered it for you, you wouldn't call the recovery person a criminal - you'd thank them.

**Our situation:**
- munotes.in stole MU's materials
- We're recovering them for the rightful owner (students)
- This is morally justified restitution
